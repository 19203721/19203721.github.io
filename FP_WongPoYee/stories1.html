“Red ribbon” mother feels powerless and tries to repair the relationship

This kind of conflict and division also bothers many families. Wong, who has two daughters, both as a “yellow ribbon” and go to protest frequently. 

Her two daughters Miki and Bobo quarreled with father because of political opinions in mid-August, both of them live in university halls now. 

Wong said, every time there was a protest, she was afraid her daughters will be arrested and felt powerless that her daughters moved out. 

“My husband is a pro-Beijing supporter, he talks to daughters that if they don't recognize themselves as a Chinese, don't come home anymore," Wong said. 

Wong describes that she doesn’t care about politics and she only hopes her children have a stable job and form a family. She never imagined that the anti-extradition bill protest will make such changes to the relationship between her and her daughters. 

She said at the very beginning, she thought the protesters were brainwashed and there was an involvement of foreign forces. "Mom can give you money. I don’t want you to do illegal things because of money." She tells her daughters but both of them were very angry. "I didn't expect you will see me like this!" Her daughter return. 

Wong says she just wants her children have a stable job.

13 Nov, many protesters gather in Sha Tin, Wong knows that the demonstrators were near her house, think that her daughters will also be there, and looking for children on the street. However, she saw hundreds of the neighborhood wearing slippers surrounded and cursed the police. 

"At that time, I realized that it wasn't just the young people hated the police but also some neighborhood who on my age." 

The riot police yelled at her and other neighbors, she was frightened and fled home. 

After returning home, a pungent smell came out from the window, and she can see the riot police fired tear gas. She coughed and closed the window. 

"After this day, I am sure my children did not collect any money to do these things. Who would like to smell tear gas like this? I used to think that the police enforced the law to arrest violent protesters, but at the scene, I found that the police use of force sometimes is excessive. " 

Therefore, she decided to find her daughters for conversation. "If protesters use the illegal method to fight for some appeal, police must stop them. Of course, sometimes the police use of force is excessive. How can they use fire? This is illegal, can it fight for what they want? But can the problem be solved by arresting them all? I don’t think so.” 

“If Carrie Lam would respond to some reasonable demands, it can prevent the young protester from becoming violent, but if you ask me how to fight for the demand peacefully, I don't know. 

" Wong tried to talk to her daughters rationally, although this cannot solve the contradiction, at least she tried to narrow the gap between them.
